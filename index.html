How this works

The problem, plainly

Your NFT is on Ethereum permanently. But the actual image and metadata files live on IPFS, kept alive by Foundation's servers. When Foundation goes dark, those servers stop. The art file can disappear. Your token still exists but points to nothing. This tool pins your files somewhere you control before that happens.

[01]

Enter your wallet address

Paste your public address or connect your wallet. We never ask you to sign anything. No transaction. Nothing spent.

[02]

We find rescuable NFTs minted to your wallet

We scan Ethereum and Base for mint records, then look for IPFS metadata and media CIDs we can back up. This finds everything: held pieces, listed pieces, and sold pieces.

[03]

Save your own copy

Your art is already preserved by the community. Click "Save all to IPFS" to add your own personal copy to Pinata — free, permanent, independent of Foundation. Your backup, your control.

[04]

Delist listed pieces if needed

Foundation's UI is offline. Use the Delist button on each listed card to cancel directly via your wallet. Each delist requires a small gas fee.

[05]

Move forward

Art safe. List on Zora or Manifold today. Or wait for canvas, a new platform being built right now with zero fees, forever.

[ security check ]

Always confirm you are on savemyart.xyz before connecting your wallet. This is the only real URL. No team member will ever DM you a link.

[ delisting: what to expect ]

Foundation's UI is offline. Any piece still listed is locked — collectors cannot buy it, and you cannot relist it anywhere else until the on-chain listing is cancelled.

The Delist button calls Foundation's original marketplace contract directly. Your wallet will show you the transaction before you confirm — nothing is signed blindly.

After delisting: the NFT returns to your wallet immediately. You are free to relist anywhere — OpenSea, SuperRare, Manifold, anywhere.

[ connecting your wallet ]

Connecting is read-only until you choose to delist. We never request token approvals, custody, or signatures beyond the individual delist transactions you initiate.

Each delist is one transaction, one piece. You can stop at any time.

[saved] or [◆ community backup] = secured. [listed] = needs delisting.
